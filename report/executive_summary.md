# Executive Summary

Russia and Eurasia remain structurally important because of energy resources, heavy industry, metals, logistics corridors and proximity to European and Asian industrial demand.

The decision problem is not whether Russia has industrial relevance. It does. The decision problem is whether an international industrial gas company can legally, financially and operationally capture that relevance under current sanctions, payment, insurance and technology-transfer constraints.

## Base Recommendation

**Russia: WAIT / AVOID under current sanctions.**

A Russia re-entry can be reconsidered only under partial easing or normalization scenarios, and only after:
- verified payment channels,
- insurance availability,
- export-control clearance,
- site-level energy tariff confirmation,
- binding customer offtake contracts.

## Near-Term Alternatives

India and Kazakhstan should remain higher-priority industrial options because they preserve Eurasian growth exposure with lower direct sanctions friction.
